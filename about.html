Imran Khan

Imran Khan, born on October 5, 1952, is a former cricketer, politician, and philanthropist from Pakistan. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest cricket players of all time, having led the Pakistani national team to its first and only Cricket World Cup victory in 1992.

After retiring from cricket, Imran Khan entered politics and founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party in 1996. His political career has been marked by a focus on anti-corruption, social justice, and development. In 2018, he became the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan, where he aimed to transform the nation through various reforms.

In addition to his political and sports career, Khan is known for his philanthropic efforts, including the founding of the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital & Research Centre in Lahore, named after his mother. His life has been a blend of sports, politics, and humanitarian work, making him a significant figure in Pakistan's history.
